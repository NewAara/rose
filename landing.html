Keeps are project-oriented schools where people form balanced professional networks applying their passions to fulfilling others’ practical needs. The disciplines practiced at Keeps are structured to cover the full breadth of humanity’s essential and cultural needs, everything from arts to engineering, cuisine to construction to clothing. Our students develop a richly-connected community. As a graduate, you will share close relationships with at least four graduates from every discipline: four clothiers who can tailor their work to your personal aesthetic, four chefs who understand your taste, four teachers, doctors, writers, guardians–friends who can address any need or desire you might have, each of them equally well connected. We fully expect world-changing projects to arise of graduate circles, and provide support for alumni to establish their own corporations, nonprofits, and even their own Keeps. Each Keep earns profits through tuition and sponsored projects, wherein apprentices collaborate with industry leaders to produce valuable products. Apprentices can help fund their education through participation in these projects. Keeps are inherently charitable. Each year, a portion of the profits from Keeps are redistributed among the others, so that the most profitable Keeps help fund those founded in under-privileged communities. `EK[] is grounded in the principle of environmental harmony. It is structured to reward Keeps with sustainable operations, and to fund projects that help restore elemental balance. ​ ​ Our Housing system rotates students between communal houses during each year of study. Every year, they meet at least one student from each craft. Students are assigned projects that help them learn how to apply the skills they are learning to the real needs of members in their house. Chefs cook for their housemates, clothiers make them clothing, designers help them with visualizing concepts. Each person learns to adapt their craft in a highly personalized way. Every year, students meet people with increasingly mature social networks. In this way, students naturally develop a harmonious community capable of succeeding in any venture. Our Graduates leave school with not only an education, but a community of richly connected professionals. `style[ every student graduates with: ~80 coworking relationships ~80 specialists for each craft `pre[ among housemate's direct connections ] a balanced network of ~1600 experts within only one degree of separation |font-family Lustria] `line[] note that these numbers can vary from Keep to Keep Alumni find themselves surrounded by support for even their most ambitious cross-disciplinary projects. Groups of friends know peers with all the skills they need to found world-changing institutions–corporations, philanthropic organizations, or even self-sustaining communities. `big[Our Community] extends well beyond the realm of education. We seek to introduce a natural way of life, one in which professional success is balanced with sustainable harmony. The highly practical nature of student projects allows Keeps to provide an invaluable education for lower tuition costs than traditional institutions. Because students actively contribute towards fulfilling each other's needs, upkeep costs are substantially lower than they would be for comparable educational facilities. We envision a society in which our social lives are not in competition with our work lives, in which communities of friends can live in self-sufficient abundance. `EK[] presents the foundation of such a society, a natural social architecture that cultivates lasting friendships and beautiful communities.
